# Lead Generation: Volume vs. Quality — The Real Answer

**Channel:** Alex Hormozi  
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@AlexHormozi  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Hormozi addresses one of the most persistent debates in outbound: 
should you optimize for volume or quality?

**His answer:**
"Both. In sequence. Quality first. Volume second. 
Most people reverse this and wonder why nothing works."

**Phase 1 — Quality first (months 1-3):**
Send to a small, perfectly targeted list (100-200 contacts).
Measure every variable: open rate, reply rate, 
objection patterns, language that resonates.
The goal is not pipeline — it's intelligence.
You are buying data about what works.

**Phase 2 — Volume second (months 4+):**
Once you have a tested message, offer, and sequence —
scale the list. Add inboxes. Increase send volume.
Now volume amplifies a system that already works.

**On why most teams fail:**
They skip Phase 1 entirely.
They build a list of 5,000 contacts on day one,
blast a generic sequence, get 0.3% reply rates,
conclude "cold email doesn't work,"
and move on.

Cold email works. Untested cold email at scale doesn't.

**On the compounding nature of outreach optimization:**
Improving reply rate from 2% to 4% is a 2x improvement.
Improving from 4% to 8% is another 2x.
Two iterations doubles pipeline.
Most teams run one iteration and give up.

**On what to measure:**
Hormozi's north star metric for outreach: 
cost per booked meeting.
Everything else is a proxy. 
What did it cost — in time, money, and messages sent — 
to get one meeting on the calendar?
Optimize that number relentlessly.
