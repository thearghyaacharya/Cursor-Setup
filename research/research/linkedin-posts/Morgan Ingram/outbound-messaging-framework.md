## Post Content

The messaging framework I teach to SDR teams at enterprise companies:

P — Problem
Start with the problem your ICP is experiencing right now.
Not your product. Their problem.
"Most [ICP title] are struggling with [specific pain]."

I — Impact
Quantify what that problem costs.
"That typically translates to [time lost / revenue missed / 
team friction]."

S — Solution hint
Point toward a solution category — not your product specifically.
"The teams solving this fastest are doing [approach]."

C — Credibility
Name a recognizable company who solved it.
"[Known company in their space] reduced [metric] by [X%] 
using this approach."

A — Ask
One micro-commitment.
"Worth a 15-minute conversation to see if there's a fit?"

The PISCA framework.
Works across cold email, LinkedIn DM, cold call openers, 
and even voicemail scripts.

One framework. Every channel.
Consistency across touches is what makes sequences feel 
like conversations instead of spam.

## Why This Was Selected

Introduces a named, multi-channel messaging framework 
(PISCA) that creates consistency across all outreach touches. 
From someone who has deployed this framework across 
enterprise SDR teams — not a theoretical model.

## Key Insight

Using the same messaging framework across every channel — 
email, LinkedIn, phone, voicemail — creates continuity that 
makes a sequence feel like a coherent conversation rather than 
repeated interruptions.
