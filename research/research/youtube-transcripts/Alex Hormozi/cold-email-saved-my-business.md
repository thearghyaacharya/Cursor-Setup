## Transcript Summary

Hormozi tells the story of using cold outreach to rescue a 
struggling gym launch — with no marketing budget, no brand, 
and a 30-day deadline to hit revenue targets.

**The situation:**
A new gym location was underperforming 6 weeks post-launch. 
Inbound was insufficient. Paid ads were too slow to scale. 
Cold outreach was the only channel with zero ramp time.

**The approach:**
Hormozi's team built a list of every business within 
a 5-mile radius of the gym — targeting HR managers and 
office managers who could bring in corporate memberships. 
500 contacts. All local. All relevant.

**On offer construction:**
The offer wasn't a standard gym membership — it was a 
corporate wellness trial: 2 weeks free for up to 10 employees, 
no commitment. The offer removed all friction because the 
risk was zero. Hormozi's principle: "Make the offer so good 
they feel stupid saying no."

**The results:**
14 corporate accounts in 3 weeks. 
87 new memberships from one outreach campaign.
The location went from break-even to profitable in 30 days.

**The transferable lesson:**
The campaign worked because of three things working together: 
hyper-local targeting (tight ICP), a friction-free offer, 
and follow-up persistence (7 touches over 14 days).
Remove any one of those three — results collapse.

**On the mindset of outreach:**
"You're not interrupting someone's day. You're offering them 
something that will improve their life. If you genuinely believe 
that — your copy sounds different. If you don't believe it — 
no framework in the world will save you."

---

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Cold outreach is the fastest pipeline channel when 
   budget is constrained — zero ramp time vs. paid ads
2. Friction-free offers dramatically increase conversion — 
   remove all risk before asking for commitment
3. Tight ICP targeting (500 hyper-relevant contacts) 
   outperforms wide targeting every time
4. 3 success factors must work simultaneously: tight ICP + 
   compelling offer + persistent follow-up
5. Genuine belief in the value you're offering changes 
   the tone of outreach in ways no template can replicate
