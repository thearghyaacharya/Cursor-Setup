# Multichannel Sequence Architecture — Email + LinkedIn + Beyond

**Channel:** QuickMail — Jeremy Chatelaine  
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@QuickMail  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jeremy covers how QuickMail approaches multichannel sequence 
design — integrating email, LinkedIn, and manual tasks 
into a single coherent outreach flow.

**On why multichannel matters now:**
"Email open rates are declining industry-wide. 
Not because email is dying — but because inboxes are more 
competitive than they've ever been. Adding LinkedIn as a 
parallel channel isn't optional for high-performance outreach anymore."

**On the QuickMail multichannel architecture:**
QuickMail allows teams to build sequences with steps across 
email, LinkedIn connection request, LinkedIn message, 
and manual tasks (call, video, direct mail). 
All in one sequence. All tracked centrally.

**Jeremy's recommended multichannel sequence:**
Touch 1 (Day 1): Email — personalized, no pitch
Touch 2 (Day 2): LinkedIn connection request — no note
Touch 3 (Day 3): Email follow-up — reference the connection
Touch 4 (Day 5): LinkedIn message — value-first, soft question
Touch 5 (Day 7): Email — social proof angle
Touch 6 (Day 9): Manual task — cold call (logged in CRM)
Touch 7 (Day 12): Email — direct ask
Touch 8 (Day 15): LinkedIn breakup message
Touch 9 (Day 15): Email breakup message

**On reply attribution in multichannel:**
One of the biggest challenges Jeremy identifies: 
which channel gets credit for the reply? 
His recommendation: track the sequence as a whole, 
but tag replies with "first channel where contact 
engaged" to understand which touch opened the door.

**On sequence fatigue:**
Jeremy notes that sequences longer than 10 touches 
see diminishing returns and increasing unsubscribe rates. 
"After 15 days and 9 touches, if they haven't replied, 
they're not ignoring you — they're telling you something 
about your offer or your targeting. Listen to that signal."

**On nurture after sequence completion:**
Contacts who complete the full sequence without replying 
go into a 90-day nurture — one email per month, 
purely value-based (insight, resource, relevant news). 
No ask. When timing changes on their end, 
you're already in their inbox.

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Multichannel sequences are now the standard — 
   email-only outreach is competitively disadvantaged
2. LinkedIn connection first (no note) creates context 
   before any message arrives
3. 9 touches over 15 days is the optimal range — 
   beyond this, unsubscribe rates rise meaningfully
4. Track reply attribution by "first engagement channel" 
   to understand what's actually opening doors
5. Non-responding contacts go into 90-day nurture — 
   not the trash. Timing changes.
