# Stop Wasting Email Preview Space on Pleasantries

**Author:** Will Allred  
**Date:** 2025-03-01  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/williamallred/  
**Engagement:** High — recurring theme in Will's content  

---

## Post Content

Your prospect doesn't want to meet with you before the holidays.

While they may not "want" to talk — they need to:
- Continue making progress toward their goals
- Unblock their team
- Solve problems

Focus on those things — not the soft things.

Cut these completely:
- "Don't want to be a pest"
- "Sorry for the persistence"
- "Just following up"
- "Hope you're well"
- "Happy holidays"

You're wasting preview space — the one thing keeping your message 
from being read in the first place.

If you feel the need to wish them well, put it at the bottom. 
Never in the opener. Never in the subject line.

---

## Why This Was Selected

Preview text (the snippet visible before opening an email) is one of the 
highest-leverage variables in open rate optimization. This post gives a 
clear, actionable rule most SDRs violate daily.

## Key Insight

The first 8-10 words of your email are a second subject line. Every word 
spent on pleasantries is a word not spent on making the prospect want to open.
