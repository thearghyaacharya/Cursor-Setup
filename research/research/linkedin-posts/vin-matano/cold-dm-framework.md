## Post Content

I booked 12 meetings last month from LinkedIn DMs alone.
No InMail credits. No automation tools. Just this framework.

The 4-part DM structure:

1. The Hook (1 sentence)
   Reference something they posted, commented on, or shared recently.
   "Your post on why SDRs should cold call first changed 
   how I think about sequencing."

2. The Bridge (1 sentence)
   Connect their world to your world — without pitching.
   "We work with SDR teams on exactly that problem."

3. The Ask (1 sentence)
   Low friction. Yes/no answer. Nothing that requires calendar access.
   "Would it be useful if I shared what's been working for our clients?"

4. The Exit (optional, 1 sentence)
   Give them an easy out — it paradoxically increases replies.
   "No worries if the timing isn't right."

Total message length: 4-5 sentences maximum.
If you're writing more than that, you're pitching, not connecting.

The goal of DM 1 is a reply. Not a meeting.
The meeting comes from the conversation.

## Key Insight

LinkedIn DMs work when they feel like a message from a human 
who actually read your content — not a sales template. 
The "easy exit" line at the end reduces pressure and 
paradoxically increases reply rates.
