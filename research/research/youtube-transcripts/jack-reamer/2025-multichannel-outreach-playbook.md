# The Multi-Channel Outreach Playbook: LinkedIn + Email + Phone

**Channel:** SalesBread — Jack Reamer  
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/jack-reamer/  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jack walks through the complete multi-channel sequence architecture 
that SalesBread runs for B2B clients across industries.

**On why single-channel fails:**
The average B2B decision-maker receives 120+ emails per day. 
LinkedIn messages arrive in a separate mental context — less 
cluttered, more conversational. Phone adds a human voice layer 
that text cannot replicate. Each channel reaches the prospect 
in a different state of mind. Using all three means you're present 
across the contexts where buying decisions are being made.

**The 21-day multi-channel sequence:**
- Day 1: LinkedIn connection request (no note)
- Day 2: LinkedIn message 1 — personalized observation, no ask
- Day 4: Cold email 1 — reference the LinkedIn connection
- Day 6: LinkedIn message 2 — soft question
- Day 8: Cold call — reference both previous touches
- Day 10: Cold email 2 — different value angle
- Day 13: LinkedIn message 3 — direct ask
- Day 17: Cold email 3 — case study or social proof
- Day 21: Breakup message across both LinkedIn and email

**On channel sequencing logic:**
LinkedIn first because it's lower friction — no inbox to fight, 
profile visible, professional context established before email arrives. 
Phone after 2 touchpoints because they now recognize the name.

**On the breakup message:**
Jack notes the Day 21 breakup message gets a 15-20% reply rate 
on its own — often from prospects who had been meaning to respond 
but hadn't prioritized it. The "I'll stop reaching out after this" 
framing creates a genuine deadline that triggers action.

**On tracking multi-channel performance:**
Jack recommends tracking reply rates by channel for each sequence 
— not just overall reply rates. This reveals which channel is 
driving conversations and allows optimization at the channel level.

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Multi-channel outreach reaches decision-makers across different 
   mental contexts — each channel serves a distinct role
2. LinkedIn first (lower friction), email second, phone after 
   name recognition is established
3. The 21-day sequence with 9 touches across 3 channels is the 
   current SalesBread standard
4. Breakup messages generate 15-20% reply rates — among the 
   highest in any sequence
5. Track reply rates by channel to optimize at the channel level
