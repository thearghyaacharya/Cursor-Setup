## Post Content

Email intelligence means you can tell your team:

"Your emails should be really short... unless they're hiring for a specific job title. 
Use Lavender to quickly look for this, and then lean on this template."

Real example from an Outreach customer:

Their email data was inconsistent — short, cold, personalized emails worked great. 
But so did really long emails. What gives?

A quick search of their template data revealed why: one rep was using a template 
that broke down a job post and explained why their tech was better than making 
another hire. A couple reps used it. The rest of the team had no idea.

Email intelligence isn't just an "email score." It's uncovering the black box 
of why certain emails work — and making that knowledge available to the whole team.

---

## Why This Was Selected

Challenges the assumption that short emails always win. Real data from inside 
Outreach's platform showing that context-specific long emails can outperform 
short ones — and that the real edge is knowing when to use which.

## Key Insight

The best outreach teams don't follow one template rule — they build intelligence 
systems that surface which email type works for which trigger, then scale that 
pattern across the whole team.
