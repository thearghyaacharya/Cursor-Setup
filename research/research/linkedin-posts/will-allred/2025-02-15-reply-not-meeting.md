## Post Content

The worst cold email I received this month:

"I'd love 15 minutes to introduce myself and show you what we do."

Nobody cares about your introduction.
Nobody has time for a generic pitch.

The psychological shift that's doubling meeting rates:

Stop asking for a meeting in the first email.
Ask for a reply.

The goal of email 1 is not a booked call.
The goal of email 1 is a response.

Use the Curiosity Gap:

Instead of: "We help companies do Y"
Write: "Other VPs in your space are seeing X trend — is that on your radar?"

Your brain hates incomplete information.
Your prospect's brain does too.
Use it.

---

## Why This Was Selected

Reframes the entire goal of a cold email — from "book a meeting" to 
"earn a reply." This mental model shift affects every element of copy: 
subject line, opener, body, and CTA. One of the most fundamental 
principles in modern cold outreach.

## Key Insight

Cold email CTA should match the temperature of the relationship — which 
at first contact is zero. Ask for a micro-commitment (a reply, an opinion, 
a yes/no) not a macro-commitment (30 minutes of their calendar).
