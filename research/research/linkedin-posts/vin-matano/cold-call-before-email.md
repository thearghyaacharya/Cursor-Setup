## Post Content

The SDRs booking the most meetings aren't better writers.
They're better sequencers.

Here's the multi-touch sequence that worked for me at Demandbase:

Day 1: LinkedIn connection request (no note)
Day 2: LinkedIn DM — hook + bridge + soft ask
Day 4: Cold email — reference the LinkedIn connection
Day 7: Cold call — mention the email + DM
Day 9: LinkedIn voice note — 30 seconds, casual and specific
Day 12: Email 2 — different angle, new trigger
Day 15: Final LinkedIn DM — honest breakup message

Key principles:

Each touch references the previous one.
("I connected with you on LinkedIn last week...")
This creates continuity — not cold re-introductions each time.

Each touch uses a different medium.
Same message on 7 channels = spam.
Different angles across 7 channels = persistence.

The breakup message on Day 15 gets some of the highest 
reply rates in the sequence.
"I've reached out a few times — clearly the timing isn't right. 
I'll leave you alone after this. If things change, here's my LinkedIn."
