## Post Content

I've reviewed thousands of sales cadences.
The ones that convert share 5 principles.
The ones that don't violate at least 3 of them.

1. Multi-channel from day one
   Email only cadences leave 60% of your pipeline untouched.
   LinkedIn + email + phone from the first week.

2. Value before ask — always
   Touch 1 should give something: an insight, a resource, 
   a relevant observation. Never a pitch on touch 1.

3. Each touch escalates the relevance
   Touch 2 should reference touch 1.
   Touch 3 should reference both.
   A cadence is a conversation arc — not a series of 
   independent cold approaches.

4. Pattern breaks at touch 4-5
   If you haven't heard back by touch 4, 
   do something unexpected.
   A voice note. A video. A direct question.
   Pattern breaks get replies that standard touches don't.

5. The breakup email is not the end
   "I'll stop reaching out" messages get replies.
   When they reply, re-enter them into a nurture sequence — 
   not another outbound cadence.
   Timing is everything. Stay in orbit.

## Key Insight

A sales cadence is a conversation arc — each touch should 
reference the previous ones and escalate relevance. 
Cadences that treat each touch as an independent cold 
approach fail because they create no continuity or narrative.
