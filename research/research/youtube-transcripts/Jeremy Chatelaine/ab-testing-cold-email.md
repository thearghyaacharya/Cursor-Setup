# How to A/B Test Cold Email Campaigns — A Data-Driven Framework

**Channel:** QuickMail — Jeremy Chatelaine  
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@QuickMail  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jeremy walks through the systematic A/B testing methodology 
he recommends for cold email campaigns — with specific guidance 
on what to test, sample sizes, and how to interpret results.

**On why most A/B tests are worthless:**
"Most people test two subject lines with 50 emails each, 
see a 2% difference, and declare a winner. 
That's not a test. That's noise with a label on it."

**On minimum sample sizes:**
For a result to be statistically meaningful at 95% confidence:
- Open rate tests: minimum 500 emails per variant
- Reply rate tests: minimum 1,000 emails per variant
Most small teams can't reach these numbers quickly — 
Jeremy recommends batching tests over 2-4 week periods 
rather than running underpowered tests daily.

**The testing hierarchy — what to test first:**
1. Subject line (highest impact on open rate)
2. Opening line / first sentence (highest impact on reply rate)
3. Offer / CTA (directly impacts meeting booking rate)
4. Email length (test short vs. long)
5. Sending time (test morning vs. afternoon by timezone)

"Test in this order. Don't test send time before you've 
optimized subject line and opening. 
Fix the biggest variable first."

**On what not to test simultaneously:**
Never change more than one variable per test. 
If you change subject line AND opening line simultaneously, 
you can't attribute results to either change.

**On interpreting results:**
Jeremy's rule: a test is only a winner if it improves 
the primary metric by more than 20%. 
A 2-3% improvement could be random variation. 
20%+ is a signal worth acting on.

**On applying learnings:**
Winning variants become the new control. 
You test against the best version you have — not the original. 
Over 6 months of systematic testing, this compounds into 
dramatically improved campaign performance.

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Most A/B tests are too small to be statistically meaningful — 
   minimum 500-1,000 emails per variant required
2. Test in order of impact: subject line → opening → offer → 
   length → send time
3. Change only one variable per test — never two simultaneously
4. Only act on improvements of 20%+ — smaller differences 
   may be noise
5. Systematic testing compounds over time — 
   6 months of proper testing transforms campaign performance
