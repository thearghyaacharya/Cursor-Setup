# AI-Powered Cold Email Personalization — The Complete Workflow

**Channel:** Jay Feldman  
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@JayFeldmanYT  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jay breaks down his exact workflow for using AI to personalize 
cold emails at scale — including the specific prompts, 
quality control process, and failure modes to avoid.

**On why most AI personalization fails:**
"Most people use AI to write the whole email. 
That's the mistake. AI writes generic at scale. 
Use AI for one thing: the first line. 
Keep the rest human-written and tested."

**The trigger hierarchy Jay uses in Clay:**
1. LinkedIn post in the last 30 days — highest relevance
2. Company news (funding, launch, expansion, hire) — high relevance
3. Job posting indicating a specific business priority — medium relevance
4. Tech stack change (via BuiltWith or similar) — medium relevance
5. No trigger available — skip this contact or use category personalization

**The AI prompt structure:**
"You are a B2B sales expert writing a cold email first line.
Contact: [Name], [Title] at [Company].
Trigger: [Specific trigger from enrichment].
Value prop: [One sentence on what we do].
Write a 1-sentence opener that references the trigger naturally 
and connects it to the value prop.
Sound human. No buzzwords. No exclamation points.
Maximum 25 words."

**On quality control:**
Jay runs every AI output through a simple test:
Does this read like something a human wrote after 
5 minutes of research? Yes → use it. No → flag for manual rewrite.
Roughly 15-20% of AI outputs get flagged and rewritten.

**On A/B testing AI vs. human first lines:**
Jay ran a 2,000-email test. AI-written first lines with 
human review: 8.3% reply rate. Fully human-written first lines: 
9.1% reply rate. The 0.8% difference doesn't justify 
the time cost of full manual writing at scale.

---

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Use AI for first lines only — not full email generation
2. Trigger quality hierarchy determines personalization depth
3. The AI prompt should specify: tone, length, no buzzwords, 
   maximum word count
4. 15-20% of AI output requires human rewrite — 
   quality control is non-optional
5. AI + human review achieves near-parity with 
   fully manual writing at a fraction of the time cost
