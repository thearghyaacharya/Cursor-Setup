## Post Content

Static lists are a dying approach to outbound.

Here's the shift: trigger-based outreach.

Instead of exporting a list of 1,000 ICPs and blasting them —
you build a system that alerts you when a prospect enters 
a "high intent" moment.

High-intent triggers:
- Company posts a job opening for a role your product serves
- Company raises a funding round
- Decision-maker publishes a LinkedIn post about a pain point
- Company switches or adds a relevant tool (trackable via BuiltWith)
- Contact changes jobs into a new ICP role

How to build it in Clay:
1. Set up a Apollo/Sales Nav saved search for your ICP
2. Connect to Clay — new contacts auto-populate daily
3. Run enrichment: funding (Crunchbase), jobs (LinkedIn), 
   news (Google Alerts API)
4. Flag rows where trigger column is populated
5. Those flagged contacts go into your "hot list" — 
   outreach within 24 hours of trigger firing

The reason this works:
Relevance = timing + context.
A trigger gives you both.

## Key Insight

The best time to reach a prospect is within 24-48 hours of 
a high-intent trigger. A system that monitors and flags those 
triggers converts significantly better than any static cadence.
