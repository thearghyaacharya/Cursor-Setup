# LinkedIn Outreach Strategy: How to Get 1 Lead Per Day

**Channel:** SalesBread — Jack Reamer  
**URL:** https://salesbread.com/linkedin-outreach-strategy/  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jack opens by establishing the core premise: LinkedIn is 277% more 
effective at generating B2B leads than Facebook and Twitter, yet most 
companies use it like a broadcast channel instead of a conversation channel.

**On profile quality before outreach:**
Jack emphasizes that before sending a single message, your profile 
must convert. He describes it as a landing page — if it doesn't 
communicate credibility and relevance immediately, no message quality 
will compensate. Key profile elements: recent photo (face takes 60% 
of frame), headline written for your ICP, banner with social proof, 
About section addressing buyer pain points.

**On connection request strategy:**
Never pitch in the connection request. The single goal is to get 
connected. Jack gives specific templates — referencing a shared 
interest, a mutual connection, or something specific from their 
LinkedIn activity. The connection request that references the prospect's 
recent blog post outperforms generic requests by a significant margin.

**On the message sequence:**
- Message 1: Value or specific observation. No ask.
- Message 2: Soft question tied to their situation.
- Message 3: Direct but low-friction ask ("Would it make sense to connect?")
- Messages 4-5: Follow-up using Fibonacci timing (1, 2, 3, 5 days)

**On the 48% positive reply ratio:**
Jack attributes this number to three things: ultra-specific first lines, 
profile quality, and sequence structure. He defines "positive reply" 
strictly — meeting requests and qualified sales inquiries only. 
Not just any reply.

**On multi-channel:**
For prospects who don't reply to LinkedIn after 5 touches, Jack moves 
to cold email — and references the LinkedIn interactions explicitly 
in the email opener. The transition from LinkedIn to email creates 
continuity that pure email sequences can't replicate.

**Key quote (paraphrased):**
"Most people know what personalization means. Very few do the work 
to actually execute it on every single message. That gap is where 
SalesBread lives."

---

## Key Insights Extracted

1. LinkedIn profile is a conversion asset — fix it before any outreach begins
2. The Fibonacci follow-up sequence creates persistence without harassment
3. A 48% positive reply ratio is achievable with ultra-personalization
4. Multi-channel (LinkedIn → email) sequences outperform single-channel
5. The goal of message 1 is a reply, not a meeting
