# The Personalization System Behind High-Converting Cold Outreach

**Channel:** Cold Outreach Podcast — Jack Reamer & Jeremy Chatelaine  
**URL:** https://coldoutreach.com/  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

This episode covers the end-to-end personalization workflow 
SalesBread uses across both LinkedIn and cold email campaigns.

**On what personalization actually means:**
Jack distinguishes between three levels:
- Level 1: Variable substitution ({{FirstName}}, {{Company}}) — 
  not personalization, just mail merge
- Level 2: Category personalization ("As a VP of Sales...") — 
  marginally better, still generic
- Level 3: Individual personalization — references something 
  specific to that exact person that only someone who researched 
  them could know

Level 3 is the only one that consistently moves reply rates.

**On building personalization at scale with AI:**
The SalesBread workflow:
1. Research assistant identifies 1-2 specific triggers per prospect
   (LinkedIn post, podcast appearance, company news, product launch)
2. AI drafts a first line referencing the trigger
3. Human reviewer approves or edits (takes ~30 seconds per line)
4. Personalized first line is mapped to {{custom_intro}} in the sequence

**On which triggers work best:**
Jack ranks triggers by effectiveness:
1. A specific opinion they published (highest relevance)
2. A result they shared publicly
3. A company milestone (funding, launch, expansion)
4. A job posting that signals a specific priority
5. A mutual connection or shared experience (lowest but still effective)

**On follow-up personalization:**
Most teams personalize touch 1 and revert to generic on touches 2-5. 
Jack argues every follow-up should reference the previous touch 
and add a new observation or angle — not just "bumping this up."

**On measuring personalization quality:**
The proxy metric Jack uses: "Would this first line make the prospect 
think 'how did they know that?' If yes — it's working. If not — 
it's still generic."

## Key Insights Extracted

1. True personalization requires individual-level research — 
   category-level personalization is not enough
2. AI-assisted personalization with human review is the 
   scalable sweet spot
3. Trigger quality hierarchy: published opinion > result > 
   milestone > job posting > mutual connection
4. Every follow-up should add a new angle — not just a bump
5. The test: would the prospect think "how did they know that?"
