# How to Build a Cold Email System That Generates Clients on Autopilot

**Channel:** Jay Feldman  
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/@JayFeldmanYT  
**Collected:** April 2026  

---

## Transcript Summary

Jay walks through the complete cold email system he used to 
build his agency to seven figures — covering every layer 
from infrastructure to copy to follow-up.

**On the tool stack:**
Jay's current stack:
- Apollo.io — list building and contact data
- Clay — enrichment and AI personalization
- Instantly — sending infrastructure and inbox rotation
- Slack — real-time reply notifications and team handoffs
- HubSpot — CRM for pipeline tracking post-reply

He emphasizes that the tool stack is less important than 
the process behind it. "You can build a $1M pipeline with 
half these tools if your ICP definition and copy are sharp."

**On domain architecture:**
Jay runs all cold outreach from domains that are not his 
primary company domain. Separate domain per campaign type. 
Each domain has 2-3 inboxes. Each inbox capped at 40 emails/day. 
Warm-up runs permanently in the background on all inboxes.

**On the AI personalization workflow:**
Clay pulls LinkedIn activity, recent posts, company news, 
and job postings for each contact. A GPT-4 prompt generates 
a custom first line for each contact referencing the most 
relevant trigger. Jay reviews 10-20% of output randomly — 
if quality is consistent, he lets the rest run.

**On sequence structure:**
Day 1: Personalized email 1 — observation + bridge + micro-ask
Day 3: Follow-up 1 — different angle, add a data point
Day 5: Follow-up 2 — short, direct, reference previous emails
Day 8: Follow-up 3 — social proof (name a client result)
Day 12: Breakup email — honest, no tricks

Total: 5 touches over 12 days. 
Jay found that beyond 5 touches, reply rate improvement 
doesn't justify the deliverability risk.

**On reply management:**
Every reply — positive or negative — gets a human response 
within 2 hours. "Negative replies are not rejections. 
They're timing mismatches. Put them in a nurture sequence 
and follow up in 90 days."

## Key Insights Extracted

1. Domain architecture is non-negotiable — 
   never use your primary domain for cold outreach
2. AI personalization at scale requires spot-check quality 
   control — review 10-20% of AI output randomly
3. 5 touches over 12 days is Jay's optimal sequence — 
   beyond 5 touches, deliverability risk outweighs reply rate gain
4. Every reply — including negative ones — deserves a 
   human response within 2 hours
5. "Negative replies are timing mismatches" — 
   put them in a 90-day nurture, not the trash
